1. The boundary escape
The first move is to draw a boundary:
Consciousness is inside the physical system, fully explained by it, and nowhere else.
Outside that boundary, nothing matters.

2. The regress escape
The second move is to deny a final boundary and accept regress:
Consciousness is explained by physical processes, which are explained by deeper physical laws, which are explained by still deeper ones, and so on.
This avoids drawing a sharp line, but it introduces a different problem.
If there is no terminating level, then there is no guarantee that the current level is metaphysically decisive.

It merely refuses to engage with them.
A rational agent does not treat unanswered questions as resolved, especially when the cost of error is terminal.
Brute facts are placeholders, not guarantees.
